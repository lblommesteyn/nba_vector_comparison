The Science Behind Player Comparisons

Our NBA Player Analogy Finder uses advanced statistical analysis and machine learning to find players who share similar playing styles across different positions and eras.

Data Collection

We analyze over 8,800 player-seasons with 50+ statistical metrics, including advanced analytics like PER, BPM, and VORP.

Statistical Analysis

Our algorithm normalizes and weights statistics to account for different eras, playing styles, and team contexts.

Intuitive Results

Get clear, explainable results that show not just who is similar, but why they're similar.

The Algorithm

Our algorithm uses a multi-dimensional approach to find the most accurate player analogies:

1

Vector Space Modeling

Each player is represented as a vector in a high-dimensional space based on their statistical profile, allowing us to calculate similarities between players.

2

Similarity Scoring

We calculate multiple similarity scores to capture different aspects of player comparison:

Score Type Weight Description
Style Transfer 30% Projects Player A's style onto Player B's context
Archetype 25% Similarity to Player A's playing style
Physical 20% Height, weight, and position matching
Position 15% Role and position compatibility
Context 10% Similarity to Player B's context
3

Result Ranking

Players are ranked based on a weighted combination of these scores, with the top matches displayed in order of similarity. Hover over any score to see the detailed breakdown.

Understanding the Results

Score Breakdown

Each result includes a match score from 0-100%. Hover over the score to see a detailed breakdown of how it was calculated, including the contribution of each component.

  • Style Transfer: How well the player embodies Player A's style in Player B's context
  • Archetype: Similarity to Player A's playing style
  • Physical: Physical attributes and position compatibility
  • Position: Role and position matching
  • Context: Similarity to Player B's context
Position and Physical Info

Each result includes the player's primary position and physical attributes to help you understand how they compare to the players in your query.

The position badge color indicates the player's primary position group:

  • PG/SG Guards
